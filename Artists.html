LEONARDO THE VINCI

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance, born on April 15, 1452, in Vinci, Italy, and he passed away on May 2, 1519, in Amboise, France. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest painters in history, known for masterpieces like the "Mona Lisa" and "The Last Supper." Da Vinci was not just a painter; he was also an engineer, scientist, inventor, anatomist, and writer. His notebooks contain a wealth of drawings and notes on topics ranging from anatomy to engineering, showcasing his insatiable curiosity and inventive mind. Some of his conceptual inventions include designs for a helicopter, a tank, and various other machines that were ahead of his time. Leonardo epitomized the Renaissance humanist ideal, and his contributions to art and science have left a lasting legacy.
